Highest priority

You are losing bookings at the finish line

Source: the site's own error_log, read directly off the server.

The log shows 112 fatal crashes of the same type — “Invalid address (From)” — every time someone submits the booking or contact form. Each one is a visitor who filled in their trip details and hit an error page instead of reaching your inbox. Even conservatively, that's dozens of real enquiries that never arrived. At typical London airport-transfer pricing, that's an estimated £1,500–£3,000 of bookings gone quiet — with zero extra traffic needed to recover it.

A second, quieter leak: the booking form's phone field only accepts an 11-digit UK number. Your five-star reviews are almost all from visitors — exactly the guests who have a foreign number and can't submit the form at all.

What your competitors do that you don't — yet

Sites currently outranking you (evchauffeurs.com, vipluxurychauffeur.com, justinchauffeurs.co.uk) all share one thing: a dedicated landing page per route, with a fixed price. People don't search “EV chauffeur” — they search “Heathrow to Central London Tesla” or “wedding car electric London.”

  • Build 3–4 route pages — Heathrow, Gatwick, weddings, cruise ports — each with the car, the fixed price, and a book button.
  • Add a WhatsApp click-to-chat button — the lowest-friction booking channel for a tourist with no UK number.
  • Send the customer a confirmation email too — right now only you receive the booking.
  • Embed your live Google rating — the reviews are strong; showing them on-site lifts conversion.
